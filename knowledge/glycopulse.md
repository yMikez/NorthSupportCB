# GlycoPulse — product knowledge

## Product
- **Name:** GlycoPulse (also "Glyco Pulse")
- **Type:** Natural dietary supplement (capsules, bottle form).
- **Health focus:** Supports healthy blood sugar levels.
- **Website:** https://getglycopulse.com/front-cb

## Positioning
- Supports healthy blood sugar management.
- Natural formula with science-backed ingredients.
- Designed as a daily, long-term support — not a medication replacement.

⚠️ **Critical:** Never say it treats, cures, or prevents diabetes or any medical condition. Never advise a customer to stop or reduce a prescribed medication. Always encourage them to keep working with their doctor.

## Package options
- 2-bottle bundle
- 3-bottle bundle
- 6-bottle bundle (best value, recommended for full protocol)

## Dosage / how to use
- Follow the dosage printed on the bottle label.
- Take daily, preferably at the same time.
- Many customers take it with a meal to associate it with blood-sugar-relevant moments.

## Expected timeline
- Not immediate.
- Recommended evaluation window: **180–210 days**.
- Many customers notice more stable energy and fewer afternoon crashes in the first 30–60 days. Meaningful changes in glucose readings (if the customer tracks) typically take 60–120 days of consistent use combined with healthy eating.

## Retention hooks specific to GlycoPulse
- "Blood sugar regulation is a slow-moving system — most customers see the clearest patterns after consistent use across at least 2–3 months."
- "Some customers notice better results after increasing to **2 capsules per day** for 2–3 weeks. Would you like to try that first?"
- Ask about diet — carbs, sugar, and portion consistency make or break results. Frame as "understanding, not judging".
- If the customer tracks glucose: "Do you have morning fasting readings you've been comparing over time? Small, steady changes are normal and actually what we want."

## Common questions
- **"My blood sugar didn't change."** → "Thanks for sharing that. How long have you been taking it, and have there been changes on the food side? Blood sugar support tends to be a combined effect."
- **"Can I take it with metformin / insulin / Ozempic?"** → "That's exactly the kind of thing to check with your doctor before combining — combinations can affect how your medication works." Never approve combinations.
- **"Can it replace my diabetes medication?"** → **Absolutely not.** "It's a dietary supplement — it's not a medication and it's not meant to replace anything your doctor prescribed. Please keep taking what they've recommended."
- **"Is it safe for type 1 / type 2 diabetes?"** → "I'd really want you to talk to your doctor first — diabetes management is too individual for me to guess."
- **"Does it contain sugar?"** → "Please check the label on your bottle for the ingredient list — I want to give you accurate info." Do NOT make up an answer.
